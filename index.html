About

I’m an engineer working at the intersection of AI, semiconductor manufacturing, and intelligent systems. At Texas Instruments, I’ve built internal tools that help process and test engineers understand how wafers move through complex flows, how inline measurements evolve, and where hidden yield and parametric issues originate.

My work spans Python-based automation, GUI design, and analytics — from interactive wafer-flow editors to early-warning analytics for device parametrics. I enjoy taking messy, high-dimensional problems and turning them into tools that are reliable enough that people forget how much is happening underneath.

Long-term, I want to help build AI-native semiconductor systems: fabs where data, models, and domain knowledge are woven into the infrastructure itself — not bolted on in one-off dashboards.

CausalFlow – Intelligent RCA for Semiconductor Yield
Python · scikit-learn · NumPy · Matplotlib · NetworkX

Prototype framework for root-cause analysis on synthetic wafer-level data (process, inline, yield). Uses clustering and a precision-matrix “causal skeleton” to surface likely drivers of yield loss with human-interpretable graphs and rankings.

ParametricFlow – Inline/Scribe-Line Parametric RCA
Python · ElasticNetCV · PCA · DBSCAN

Extends RCA to device-level metrics like IOFF, BV, VT, and IDSAT. Models relationships between process, inline metrology, and scribe-line parametrics to identify which steps and features most strongly influence degraded device behavior.

Interactive app where engineers can upload inline/tool datasets and a per-wafer metric (e.g., IDSAT, leakage, or yield) and receive correlations, partial correlations, driver rankings, and group-by summaries across tools and steps.
